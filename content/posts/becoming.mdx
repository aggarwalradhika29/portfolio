## on becoming someone i would’ve looked up to

when i was younger, i had a very specific image of the kind of person i wanted to become. she was smart. independent. disciplined. calm under pressure. building something meaningful. she had her life together.

at least, that’s how i imagined it.

back then, i thought becoming her would feel obvious. like one day i’d wake up and suddenly feel ready. confident. sorted.

but growth doesn’t announce itself like that.

it happens quietly.

you start taking responsibility for your choices. you learn how to manage your time. you show up to work even when you don’t feel like it. you invest in your health. you think about your future seriously. and slowly, without realizing it, you become someone younger-you would have admired.

not perfect. not fearless. not effortlessly confident.

just consistent.

there are still doubts. still days where i feel behind. still moments where i compare myself to people ahead of me. but if i pause and look at it from a distance — i’m working in spaces i once found intimidating. i’m handling things i once thought were “adult problems.” i’m building skills i once only read about. i’m not finished. but i’m not the girl who was just hoping anymore either.

and maybe that’s what growth really looks like. not a dramatic transformation. just small, daily decisions that stack up over time. i don’t know if i fully feel like the person i imagined. but sometimes, when i look at my life quietly, i think younger me would be proud.

and that feels like a good place to be.
