## on skincare, eczema, and thinking i knew everything

i have mild eczema. it’s not dramatic. it doesn’t look alarming. but it’s always there — dry patches, random flare-ups, skin that reacts when it feels like it.

for a long time, i treated it like a problem i could outsmart.

i went deep. ingredient lists became bedtime reading. i knew what ceramides did. what niacinamide did. what hyaluronic acid did. i understood skin barrier, ph levels, inflammation, fragrance triggers (funny enough i used to work for a perfume brand at the same time?).

i read so much about ingredients and skin type relationships that my coworkers would literally walk up to me and say, “i have oily acne-prone skin with pigmentation, suggest a routine.” “my skin barrier is damaged, what should i use?”

and i would. confidently. somewhere in all of this, i slowly started becoming my own dermatologist.

and honestly? that’s where i messed up.

my routine became layered. 7–8 steps. morning and night. cleanser. toner. essence. serum. another serum. calming ampoule. barrier cream. sunscreen.

it felt productive. it felt like i was doing something serious for my skin.

if i was doing more, my skin should improve… right?

but instead, my skin was irritated. confused. sensitive in a new way. i was “treating” problems i probably created. what i didn’t want to admit was that i was overcomplicating it.

the real shift didn’t come from adding another serum. it came from removing almost everything.

now my routine is almost boring.

cleanser. moisturizer. sunscreen in the morning.

at night, maybe one treatment(rarely though) if my skin actually needs it. not because it’s trending. not because someone on social media swears by it. just because my skin feels like it needs support.

and my skin looks better now than it did during my skincare-enthusiast era.

but the biggest change wasn’t just products.

i started paying attention to my diet. not in a strict way — just noticing patterns. i started working out more consistently. not for weight loss, but for stress. because stress shows up on my skin before it shows up anywhere else.

and then i finally did the thing i should’ve done earlier.

i visited a dermatologist. sitting there and hearing, “your barrier is compromised. simplify.” felt… humbling.

all that research. all those ingredient breakdowns. and the advice was basically: stop doing so much. there’s a difference between being informed and trying to become your own skin doctor.

i still love skincare. i still read ingredients. i still give people around me suggestions (i can’t help it).

but now i respect my skin more.

more layers don’t mean more care. more products don’t mean more progress. sometimes healing looks like doing less.

and for someone who loves optimizing everything, that was strangely a hard lesson to learn.
