## slow life (or whatever this phase is)

for a good time, i thought being busy meant i was doing well.

if my calendar was full, if i was tired by the end of the day, if i had 10 tabs open in my head - i felt important. productive. on track. and honestly, on paper, everything still looked fine. i was working. i was learning. i was building something. i was trying to become better. but somewhere in between all this trying, i realized i wasnt actually sitting with my life. i was just moving through it.

fast.

my days started early, and before i even got out of bed, my mind was already running - tasks, messages, goals, the next version of me iwas chasing. by the time the day ended, iwas exhausted in a way that sleep didnt fix. not because i did too much. but because i never paused.

slow life, for me, isnt quitting everything and disappearing to the mountains. its smaller than that. softer.

its drinking my morning coffee/tea without scrolling. its taking a short walk without headphones. its finishing one thing properly instead of half-doing five. its calling my grandparents without watching the clock. its choosing depth over speed.

ive noticed how fast i do everything - eating too quickly, replying too quickly, even thinking too quickly. as if life is something to clear off a to-do list.

and i dont want to live like iam constantly catching up.

some days, i still rush. i still scroll. i still measure my worth in output. i still feel behind. but on the days i slow down, even a little, something shifts. i feel more present. more inside my own life instead of watching it from a distance. the best parts of my day lately havent been the big achievements. they've been the quiet ones no one sees.

cooking without hurrying. watching the sky change color in the evening. sitting with a thought instead of escaping it. nothing dramatic. just real. slow life doesnt mean i stop growing. it just means i stop running so hard that i leave myself behind.

maybe its not about doing less. maybe its about feeling more.

and right now, that feels enough.
