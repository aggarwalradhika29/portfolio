## on being introverted in a loud world

i’m an introvert.

not the mysterious, silent-in-the-corner kind. not the “i hate people” kind either.

just the kind that feels tired after too much noise.

for a long time, i thought this was something i needed to fix.

in school, the loud ones were confident. in college, the visible ones got noticed. at work, the ones who spoke first seemed more certain.

and i was always the one who thought first. i process slowly. i observe more than i interrupt. i rehearse sentences in my head before saying them out loud.

sometimes by the time i’m ready to speak, the topic has already moved on. and i sit there thinking, “i should’ve said that.”

it’s strange — because in my head, i have so much to say. i have opinions. ideas. strong ones, even. but they feel safer when they’re written. structured. thought through.

being introverted isn’t about not liking people. i love deep conversations. one-on-one talks. long voice notes. i just struggle with noise. with constant talking. with performing. the world rewards speed. quick answers. quick jokes. quick confidence.

but this thing in my head doesn’t work like that. it works quietly. in layers.

for a while, i tried to become louder. forced myself to speak more. react faster. fill silence. it worked sometimes. but it also left me drained in a way i couldn’t explain.

now i’m trying something different.

i’m not trying to become loud. i’m trying to become clear.

if i don’t speak first, that’s okay. if i need time to think, that’s okay. if i prefer depth over volume, that’s okay too.

maybe introversion isn’t a flaw. maybe it’s just a different rhythm.

and maybe i don’t need to change my volume. maybe i just need to trust my pace.
